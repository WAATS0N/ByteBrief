Existing systems enable news aggregation through platforms such as Google News, Flipboard, and Feedly by collecting articles via RSS feeds or APIs chosen by users.
Most platforms integrate AI-based summarization and recommendation engines, but these provide only generic summaries and lack misinformation detection or sentiment-aware delivery.
Particularly with personalization, engines like SmartNews and News360 remember user history to suggest news but do not adapt to real-time contextual interests.
The systems mostly provide breaking news and ranked feeds but fail to cross-reference multiple outlets or highlight credibility and bias markers for users.
A considerable number of news aggregators rely on web or mobile apps, requiring continuous internet and offering limited scalability for real-time scraping, translation, or bias detection.

Proposed System
The system will use intelligent web scrapers to gather articles from trusted sources across politics, business, entertainment, technology, and sports, managed by an AI agent that prioritizes sources and adapts to website changes.
It will support multilingual ingestion and translation for wider global reach, with backend pipelines combining scraping and NLP analysis and a responsive frontend delivering clutter-free news briefs.
Scalability will be ensured through microservices and cloud integration, while a clean interface will let readers browse real-time news by category with personalized recommendations.
Interactive features such as topic filters, trending insights, and sentiment-aware highlights will help users navigate efficiently.
To improve reliability, the system will cross-reference outlets to flag inconsistencies and bias, while NLP models will extract entities, summarize content, and detect sentiment and tone.
